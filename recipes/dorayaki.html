Dorayaki

Description

By giving a spin on an 800-year-old classic Japanese dish with the help of Kojirō Shinomiya, Megumi enhanced the flavor of the apple with an apple butter castella and an apple confiture, white bean paste filling. The combined flavor of this dish is like a punch packed with twice the power!!!

Steps

  1. Make batter by mixing egg with sugar, honey, mirin, then flour and baking soda.
  2. Rest the batter in the fridge for 30 minutes. This will weaken the gluten formation which makes the pancake fluffier.
  3. Heat a non-stick frying pan to 180°C / 356°F, oil and wipe it off.
  4. Drop the batter onto a frying pan to form an 8cm / 3⅛” circle and cook until many bubbles come up.
  5. Flip the pancake and cook for 15 seconds.
  6. Transfer the pancake to a plate and cover with a wet towel/kitchen paper to prevent the pancakes from drying out.
  7. Spread anko on a pancake and make a sandwich with another pancake.
